Are You Filling Your Belly or Feeding Your Body?

Malaysia's Nutritional Facts

Do you ever feel fatigued or low on energy and you simply can't tell why?
The problem often persists because the food you’re eating isn’t actually fueling your body.

Let's take a closer look at Malaysia's diet and the health trends linked to it!

4 out of 10

highest causes of death in Malaysia are related to diet and lifestyle.

Among these, diabetes, which is largely influenced by diet, was particularly concerning. Diabetes ranked high for three years, before showing a slight decline in 2021. In addition, we see that there were nearly 60% female reported to be diabetic around 10% more than male.


However, when compared globally, Malaysia's diabetes prevalence in 2021 was the highest among Southeast Asian countries, even surpassing neighboring regions. This suggests a critical need to reassess dietary habits in the country.

Nearly 50% Most Consumed food is unhealthy


Surprisingly, 5 of the 12 most popular foods in Malaysia, are processed and unhealthy.



Next, we'll compare the nutrition of these five unhealthy foods with a healthier option, canned sardines.

We observe a significant difference: the unhealthy foods are high in carbohydrates but deficient in protein and essential vitamins. Conversely, canned sardines, commonly available at local mamak stalls, offer a healthier alternative with vital nutrients.

This trend underscores the importance for Malaysians to transition towards healthier dietary choices.


Adding more real, vegetables, fruits, and whole foods into the daily diet can significantly change nutrient intake. The diagram below demonstrates how incorporating these whole foods ensures a balance of both macro and micronutrients, which is vital for better health outcomes and reducing diet-related diseases like diabetes.
